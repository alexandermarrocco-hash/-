Universal Brewing Guides

eco

Gongfu Cha Method

The traditional Chinese style focusing on multiple short steepings to experience the flavor evolution.

  • 100ml Vessel (Gaiwan)
  • 5-7g of Tea Leaves
  • 10-30s Infusions
coffee

Western Style

The everyday brewing method using larger vessels and longer steeping times for a full-bodied cup.

  • 300-500ml Teapot
  • 3-5g of Tea Leaves
  • 3-5 Minute Infusion
ac_unit

Cold Brewing

Extract the natural sweetness without any bitterness by steeping in cold water over a long period.

  • 1L Pitcher
  • 10-15g of Tea Leaves
  • 6-12h in Fridge

Tea Varieties & Oxidation

Understanding Oxidation

Most people don't realize that all tea—Green, Oolong, Black, and White—comes from the same plant: *Camellia Sinensis*. The difference lies in how much the leaf is allowed to "breathe" or oxidize after harvest.

0-5%

Green Tea

15-85%

A Brief History of Tea

"Water is the mother of tea, a teapot its father, and fire the teacher." – Ancient Proverb

2737 BC – The Discovery

Legend has it that the Chinese Emperor Shen Nong discovered tea when some wild leaves fell into his pot of boiling water.

618-907 AD – The Tang Dynasty

Tea became the national drink of China. Lu Yu wrote "The Classic of Tea", the first definitive book on the subject.

1610 – Arrival in Europe

The Dutch East India Company brought the first commercial shipment of tea to Europe, changing global trade forever.
